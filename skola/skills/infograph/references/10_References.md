# References

Authoritative sources for infographic design principles, accessibility standards, and technical communication best practices.

## Information Design & Visualization

### Edward Tufte
* **"The Visual Display of Quantitative Information"** (1983)
  - Data-ink ratio principle
  - Chartjunk elimination
  - Small multiples
  - Visual integrity
* **"Envisioning Information"** (1990)
  - Layering and separation
  - Color and information
  - Micro/macro readings
* **Key principle**: "Above all else show the data"

### Nielsen Norman Group (NN/g)
* **[Information Visualization Guidelines](https://www.nngroup.com/topic/information-visualization/)**
  - User-centered data visualization
  - Reading patterns (F-pattern, Z-pattern)
  - Cognitive load management
  - Interaction design for dashboards
* **[Visual Hierarchy Principles](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/visual-hierarchy/)**
  - Size, color, contrast, alignment
  - Gestalt principles application
  - Proximity and grouping

## Accessibility Standards

### WCAG 2.2 (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines)
* **[Level AA Conformance](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/quickref/?currentsidebar=%23col_overview&levels=aaa)**
  - **1.4.3 Contrast (Minimum)**: 4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text
  - **1.4.1 Use of Color**: Color is not the only visual means of conveying information
  - **1.4.11 Non-text Contrast**: 3:1 for UI components and graphical objects
  - **1.1.1 Non-text Content**: Provide text alternatives for visual content
* **[Understanding Accessibility](https://www.w3.org/WAI/fundamentals/accessibility-intro/)**

### WebAIM

* **[Color Contrast Guide](https://webaim.org/articles/contrast/)**

## Cognitive Science & Learning

### Cognitive Load Theory (John Sweller)
* **Intrinsic load**: Complexity of the material itself
* **Extraneous load**: How information is presented
* **Germane load**: Processing that leads to learning
* **Application**: Progressive disclosure, chunking, worked examples

### Miller's Law
* **"The Magical Number Seven, Plus or Minus Two"** (1956)
* Working memory capacity: 5-9 items
* **Application**: Limit information chunks to 3-7 for optimal retention

### Dual Coding Theory (Allan Paivio)
* Combining visual and verbal information improves learning
* **Application**: Text + diagrams together strengthen understanding

## Recommended Reading Order

For someone new to technical infographic design:

1. **Start**: Nielsen Norman Group articles (free, practical)
2. **Accessibility**: WCAG 2.2 AA quick reference + WebAIM contrast checker
3. **Design system**: Choose one (Material/Carbon) and study color + typography
4. **Theory**: Tufte's "Visual Display" or summaries of his principles
5. **Practice**: Explore Information is Beautiful for inspiration
6. **Validation**: Use WCAG checklist and teach-back tests

These references form the foundation of the infographic framework presented in this skill.
