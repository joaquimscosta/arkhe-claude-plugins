## SaaS Metrics Framework

When analyzing a SaaS idea, evaluate these key metrics:

- **MRR/ARR**: Monthly/Annual Recurring Revenue — the primary health metric
- **Churn rate**: Monthly customer churn; healthy SaaS targets < 5% monthly, < 2% for enterprise
- **NRR (Net Revenue Retention)**: Revenue from existing customers including expansion; best-in-class > 120%
- **LTV:CAC ratio**: Lifetime Value to Customer Acquisition Cost; target > 3:1
- **Payback period**: Months to recover CAC; target < 12 months for SMB, < 18 for enterprise
- **Gross margin**: SaaS typically 70-85%

## Pricing Models

- **Freemium**: Free tier + paid upgrades (Slack, Notion model) — good for PLG, requires large free base
- **Per-seat**: Price per user per month (most common) — predictable, scales with team size
- **Usage-based**: Pay per API call, storage, or compute (AWS, Twilio model) — aligns cost with value
- **Flat-rate tiers**: Good/Better/Best with feature gates — simple to understand
- **Hybrid**: Base subscription + usage overages (increasingly common)

## Growth Benchmarks

- **PLG (Product-Led Growth)**: Self-serve signup, free trial/freemium, viral loops — lower CAC, higher volume
- **Sales-Led Growth**: Enterprise sales team, demos, contracts — higher CAC, higher ACV
- **Healthy growth rates**: 3x at $1M ARR, 2x at $10M ARR, 1.5x at $50M ARR (T2D3 framework)
- **Rule of 40**: Growth rate % + profit margin % should exceed 40%

## Competitive Landscape Patterns

- Feature parity is common — differentiate on UX, integrations, or vertical focus
- Platform risk: building on top of another platform (Salesforce, Shopify) creates dependency
- Open-source alternatives may commoditize core functionality
- Vertical SaaS (industry-specific) often has better unit economics than horizontal SaaS
