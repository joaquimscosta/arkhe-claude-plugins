## Economic Context

- **Population**: ~590,000 (Cape Verde) + 200,000-265,000 diaspora (primarily US, Portugal, France)
- **GDP**: ~$2.2B USD
- **Remittances**: ~$316M annually (~12% of GDP) — a critical economic lifeline
- **Currency**: Cape Verdean Escudo (CVE), pegged to EUR at fixed rate (1 EUR = 110.265 CVE)
- **Key economic sectors**: Tourism, services, remittances, fisheries
- **Mobile penetration**: 115.8% (multiple SIMs per person)
- **Internet penetration**: ~70% and growing

## Regulatory Landscape

- **Central Bank**: Banco de Cabo Verde (BCV) — oversees all financial services
- **Key legislation**: Legislative Decrees 7/8/9 of 2018 (financial regulation framework)
- **Tax authority**: DNRE (Direção Nacional de Receitas do Estado) — e-Fatura electronic invoicing system
- **Data protection**: Cape Verde's data protection law (Lei de Proteção de Dados Pessoais)
- **Foreign exchange**: BCV controls FX operations; the CVE-EUR peg simplifies EUR corridor but USD requires conversion

## Payment Infrastructure

- **SISP (Sociedade Interbancária e Sistemas de Pagamentos)**: National interbank payment network
- **Vinti4**: SISP's ATM and POS network — the primary electronic payment system
- **Mobile money**: Growing but not yet dominant; Unitel T+ Money, CVMóvel
- **Banking**: Major banks include BCA, BCN, BAI CV, Caixa Económica
- **Cash dependence**: Still significant, especially on outer islands and among older populations

## Diaspora Demographics (US Focus)

- **Total US diaspora**: 200,000-265,000 Cape Verdeans and descendants
- **Primary concentrations**:
  - Massachusetts: Brockton (16% of population), New Bedford, Boston
  - Rhode Island: Providence, Pawtucket, East Providence
  - Connecticut: Bridgeport, Waterbury
- **Remittance corridor**: US→Cape Verde estimated at $80-95M annually
- **Cultural identity**: Strong Cape Verdean identity, Kriolu language, community organizations (e.g., Cape Verdean Association of Brockton)
- **Communication preferences**: WhatsApp dominant, Portuguese and Kriolu languages, Facebook community groups

## Competitive Landscape (Remittance Corridor)

- **Western Union**: ~50% market share, extensive pickup network in Cape Verde
- **MoneyGram**: ~27% share, available at banks and agents
- **Wise (TransferWise)**: Low fees (~2%), bank transfer only, limited Cape Verde payout options
- **Remitly, WorldRemit**: Digital-first, competitive fees, growing presence
- **Informal channels**: Estimated 40% of remittances travel informally (hand-carry, hawala-like systems)

## Cultural & UX Considerations

- **Language**: Portuguese (official), Cape Verdean Kriolu (widely spoken), English (diaspora)
- **Trust**: Personal relationships and community endorsement are critical for adoption
- **Cash-out**: Many recipients prefer or need cash; mobile-only solutions may exclude older populations
- **Island logistics**: Cape Verde has 10 islands (9 inhabited) — service coverage varies significantly
- **Seasonal patterns**: Remittances spike around holidays (Christmas, Carnaval) and family events
